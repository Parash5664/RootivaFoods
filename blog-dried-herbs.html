Dried herbs & spices: flavour first, naturally

Dried herbs and spices concentrate plant flavours while retaining many of their aromatic oils and phytonutrients. When used well, they brighten dishes and reduce the need for excessive salt, sugar or artificial additives.

Health & kitchen benefits

  • Flavor boost: a small pinch of dried herb delivers a layered aroma and depth.
  • Cleaner cooking: using herbs helps cut back on salt and processed seasonings.
  • Convenience: ready-to-use, long-lasting, and travel-friendly.

How to use dried herbs effectively

For best results, add hardy dried herbs (rosemary, thyme) early in cooking to release flavour; add delicate herbs (parsley, basil) at the end. Rehydrate in warm liquid for softer textures.

Practical uses & recipes

  • Roast seasoning: mix 1 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp onion powder, 1/2 tsp dried rosemary, salt and pepper — rub on vegetables before roasting.
  • Herb vinaigrette: 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tbsp vinegar, 1/2 tsp dried oregano, 1/2 tsp dried basil, 1/4 tsp onion powder — whisk and dress salads.
  • Quick soup boost: add 1 tsp mixed dried herbs to broth and simmer for 5 minutes to deepen flavour.

Storage tips

Keep dried herbs in airtight jars away from light and heat. Replace every 12–18 months for peak aroma. Label jars with purchase dates to maintain freshness.
